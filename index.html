About Me

Growing up in Nepal’s forests sparked my passion for trees. After forestry degrees in Nepal, the UK, and Germany—and conservation work with USAID’s Hariyo Ban Program—I now research plant ecology in the southern Appalachians as a Clemson PhD candidate. My projects span long-term vegetation plots, dendroecology, and dendrometers, and I plan to apply these skills to protect Himalayan forests through international collaboration.

Research Areas

Long-term vegetation dynamics · Tree-ring ecology · Daily growth monitoring · Climate-tree interactions
