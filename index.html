About Me

Growing up surrounded by the forests of Nepal, I’ve always felt most at home among the trees. That connection led me to study forestry (BSc in Nepal, MSc in the UK & Germany) and work on conservation projects back home with the USAID-funded Hariyo Ban Program (CARE Nepal | NEEDS Nepal) — focusing on biodiversity, climate resilience, and community-based forest management.

Now, as a PhD researcher at Clemson University, I study plant ecology in the southern Appalachians – Great Smoky Mountains National Park, with my advisor Dr Jason D. Fridley. I am excited by research that explores tree ecology and conservation through long-term vegetation dynamics in ATBI plots, dendroecology (tree rings and annual/seasonal growth), and dendrometers (daily tree growth). But no matter where my work takes me, my heart stays rooted in Nepal’s forests. My goal is to return with the skills to better protect them and foster international collaborative forestry research.
