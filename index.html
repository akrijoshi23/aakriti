About Me

Growing up in the forests of Nepal sparked my lifelong passion for trees. After forestry degrees in Nepal, the UK, and Germany—and conservation work with USAID’s Hariyo Ban Program—I am now a PhD researcher at Clemson University studying plant ecology in the southern Appalachians. My work spans long-term vegetation plots, dendroecology, and dendrometers, and I hope to bring these skills home to protect Nepal’s forests through international collaboration.
